# ToolRet: Benchmarking Tool Retrieval for Large Language Models

**Source**: [arXiv 2503.01763](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.01763) · 2025-03-03

## Key takeaways

- First large-scale tool retrieval benchmark: 7.6k tasks, 43k tools
- Even best IR models (NV-embed-v1) achieve only 33.83 nDCG@10
- Tool retrieval quality directly impacts end-to-end task pass rate
- ToolRet-train dataset (200k+) substantially improves IR model performance

## Core claims

This paper reveals a critical issue: IR models performing well on traditional benchmarks exhibit poor performance on tool retrieval tasks. The key challenges are lower term overlap between queries and tools, and task shift from information-seeking to tool retrieval. The study shows low retrieval quality degrades tool-use LLM pass rate by 10.1%.

## Notable quotes

> "Surprisingly, even the models with strong performance in conventional IR benchmarks, exhibit poor performance on ToolRet."

> "This low retrieval quality degrades the task pass rate of tool-use LLMs."
