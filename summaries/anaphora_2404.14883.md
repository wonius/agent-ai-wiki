# Language in vivo vs. in silico: Size matters but Larger Language Models still do not comprehend language on a par with humans

**Source**: [arXiv 2404.14883](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.14883) · 2024-04-22

## Key takeaways

- Tests three LLMs (Bard 137B, ChatGPT-3.5 175B, ChatGPT-4 1.5T) on grammaticality judgment tasks featuring anaphora, center embedding, comparatives, and negative polarity
- ChatGPT-4 achieves 80% accuracy vs. humans 76% — but only outperforms humans on grammatical sentences
- LLMs waver more than humans (12.5% vs. 9.6% oscillating answer likelihood)
- Model size helps but scaling alone cannot make LLMs human-like in grammaticality sensitivity
- Three critical differences identified: (i) type of evidence, (ii) poverty of the stimulus, (iii) semantic hallucinations due to impenetrable linguistic reference

## Core claims

This paper investigates whether model scaling can bridge the gap between human and LLM language comprehension. While larger models perform better, they still lack human-like sensitivity to (un)grammaticality — suggesting scaling alone cannot fix this issue.
