# R-matrix calculations for opacities: III. Plasma broadening of autoionizing resonances

**Source**: [arXiv 2308.14870](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.14870) · 2023-08-28

## Key takeaways

- General formulation for plasma broadening of autoionizing (AI) resonances in photoionization cross sections
- Uses R-matrix data for iron ions (Fe XVII, Fe XVIII, Fe XIX) to demonstrate physical mechanisms
- Considers electron collisions, ion microfields (Stark), thermal Doppler effects, core excitations, and free-free transitions
- Significant broadening and shifting of AI resonances as a function of temperature and density
- Redistribution of AI resonance strengths preserves integrated strengths while dissolving into bound-free continua

## Core claims

This paper presents a computational methodology to incorporate electron impact broadening in a generally applicable manner for laboratory and astrophysical plasma sources. It addresses how autoionization profiles are inherently asymmetric (described by Fano formula) and how plasma perturbations affect these profiles.
